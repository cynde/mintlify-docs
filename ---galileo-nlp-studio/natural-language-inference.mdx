# Natural Language Inference

<a class="underline underline-offset-2 text-primary hover:text-primary-700 transition-colors " href="http://nlpprogress.com/english/natural_language_inference.html">Natural Language Inference (NLI)</a>, also known as Recognizing Textual Entailment (RTE), is a sequence classification problem, where given two (short, ordered) documents -- <code class="py-[1px] px-1.5 min-w-[1.625rem] inline-flex justify-center items-center ring-1 ring-inset ring-dark/1 bg-dark/[0.06] rounded text-dark/8 dark:ring-light/1 dark:bg-light/1 dark:text-light/7 text-[.875em] leading-[calc(max(1.20em,1.25rem))]">premise</code> and <code class="py-[1px] px-1.5 min-w-[1.625rem] inline-flex justify-center items-center ring-1 ring-inset ring-dark/1 bg-dark/[0.06] rounded text-dark/8 dark:ring-light/1 dark:bg-light/1 dark:text-light/7 text-[.875em] leading-[calc(max(1.20em,1.25rem))]">hypothesis</code>, the task is to determine the inference relation between them.

Samples are classified into one of the three labels depending on whether a <code class="py-[1px] px-1.5 min-w-[1.625rem] inline-flex justify-center items-center ring-1 ring-inset ring-dark/1 bg-dark/[0.06] rounded text-dark/8 dark:ring-light/1 dark:bg-light/1 dark:text-light/7 text-[.875em] leading-[calc(max(1.20em,1.25rem))]">hypothesis</code> is true (entailment), false (contradiction), or undetermined (neutral) given a <code class="py-[1px] px-1.5 min-w-[1.625rem] inline-flex justify-center items-center ring-1 ring-inset ring-dark/1 bg-dark/[0.06] rounded text-dark/8 dark:ring-light/1 dark:bg-light/1 dark:text-light/7 text-[.875em] leading-[calc(max(1.20em,1.25rem))]">premise</code>. Here's an example:

```
Premise: A man inspects the uniform of a figure in some East Asian country.

Premise: A man inspects the uniform of a figure in some East Asian country.

Hypothesis: The man is sleeping.

Hypothesis: The man is sleeping.

Label: contradiction

Label: contradiction









Premise: An older and younger man smiling.

Premise: An older and younger man smiling.

Hypothesis: Two men are smiling and laughing at the cats playing on the floor.

Hypothesis: Two men are smiling and laughing at the cats playing on the floor.

Label: neutral

Label: neutral









Premise: A soccer game with multiple males playing.

Premise: A soccer game with multiple males playing.

Hypothesis: Some men are playing a sport.

Hypothesis: Some men are playing a sport.

Label: entailment
Label: entailment
```

<strong class="font-bold">Note</strong>: For NLI you must combine the <code class="py-[1px] px-1.5 min-w-[1.625rem] inline-flex justify-center items-center ring-1 ring-inset ring-dark/1 bg-dark/[0.06] rounded text-dark/8 dark:ring-light/1 dark:bg-light/1 dark:text-light/7 text-[.875em] leading-[calc(max(1.20em,1.25rem))]">premise</code> and <code class="py-[1px] px-1.5 min-w-[1.625rem] inline-flex justify-center items-center ring-1 ring-inset ring-dark/1 bg-dark/[0.06] rounded text-dark/8 dark:ring-light/1 dark:bg-light/1 dark:text-light/7 text-[.875em] leading-[calc(max(1.20em,1.25rem))]">hypothesis</code> documents for logging. We recommend joining the document text with a separator such as <code class="py-[1px] px-1.5 min-w-[1.625rem] inline-flex justify-center items-center ring-1 ring-inset ring-dark/1 bg-dark/[0.06] rounded text-dark/8 dark:ring-light/1 dark:bg-light/1 dark:text-light/7 text-[.875em] leading-[calc(max(1.20em,1.25rem))]">&lt;&gt;</code> to help visualization in the Galileo console.
